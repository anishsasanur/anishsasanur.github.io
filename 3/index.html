A4: Blend the Images into a Mosaic

In order to create these mosaics I had a multiple step process:

  1. I took 2-3 pictures that I would be stitching together into a mosaic, within these pictures I had some overlapping areas so I could correspond points
  1. I calculating point correspondences by matching key features between the left to center images and the right to center images. Then I got an output of matching points
  1. I computed homographies mapping each image to the plane of the center image using the formulas and equations mentioned earlier.
  1. I created a larger canvas for the entire mosaic, by checking the bounds of where the homography would transform the corners of the additional images
  1. I then applied the homography onto the left and right images in order to get them into the right perspective for the mosaic
  1. I used Nearest neighbor interpolation here since the speed of the calculations tended to be higher on average
  1. Once I had all of the images on the canvas, I used weighted average blending to blend the seams and edges cleaner, however there were still some unavoidable edge artifacts due to changes in lighting depending on the perspective.
