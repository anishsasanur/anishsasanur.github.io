Part 2: Applications

2.1: The unsharp mask filter is essentially subtracting a lower frequency or blurrier layer of the image from the original, and this isolates the high frequency features from the image. Then this high frequency layer is added back onto the original image making it appear sharper. After tweaking around with the scale value for sharpening, I noticed that using too high of a value, overides the original image with too many high frequencies, leading the image to consist mostly of just edges.
